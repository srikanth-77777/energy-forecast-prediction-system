SLIDE 2 — Problem Statement
- Urban energy grids are growing more complex
- Poor load estimation leads to wasted fuel (overestimate) or blackouts (underestimate)
- Traditional methods rely on simple historical averages
- Most ML research over-focuses on model architecture, ignoring data quality
- Our thesis: Feature engineering matters more than model choice

SLIDE 3 — Objectives
- Build a 23-feature engineering pipeline capturing temporal, seasonal, and behavioral patterns
- Train and compare 5 ML models across 5 real-world regional datasets
- Use leak-free evaluation with GridSearchCV + TimeSeriesSplit
- Deploy a production-grade interactive forecasting dashboard
- Demonstrate that data-centric approaches outperform model-centric complexity

SLIDE 4 — Literature Review Summary
- Early: ARIMA/SARIMA models (stationary assumptions, limited nonlinearity)

- Deep Learning: LSTM, CNN-LSTM (complex but not always better)
- Gap: Few studies do multi-region evaluation, rigorous cross-validation, or deploy interactive systems
- Our contribution: Data-centric pipeline + 5 regions + deployed dashboard

SLIDE 7 — Feature Engineering (23 Features)
Show 6 categories in a visual grid:
1. Basic Temporal (6): hour, day_of_week, month, quarter, day_of_year, week_of_year
2. Cyclical Encoding (6): sin/cos transforms for hour, day_of_week, month — preserves circular continuity
3. Binary Flags (5): is_weekend, is_peak, is_summer, is_winter, is_holiday
4. Historical Lags (2): lag_24h (same hour yesterday), lag_168h (same hour last week)
5. Rolling Statistics (3): rolling_mean_24h, rolling_std_24h, rolling_mean_168h — shifted to prevent leakage
6. Interaction (1): hour × weekend — captures different daily curves on weekends vs weekdays

SLIDE 8 — Data Leakage Discovery (IMPORTANT SLIDE - highlight this)
- Initial features included lag_1h, lag_2h, lag_1h_diff
- Result: R² = 0.9999 (seemed perfect!)
- Problem: Demand barely changes hour-to-hour, so model just memorized "same as last hour"
- This is DATA LEAKAGE — the model had access to information it wouldn't have in real forecasting
- Fix: Removed short-term lags, shifted rolling windows
- New honest R²: ~0.86 (genuine predictive ability)
- Lesson: Always verify what information is available at inference time

SLIDE 9 — Models Used

4. XGBoost — Optimized gradient boosting with regularization
5. LightGBM — Histogram-based splitting, fastest training time
- All tuned via GridSearchCV with 3-fold TimeSeriesSplit (leak-free cross-validation)
